Graphical analysis of motion¶

Equations of one dimentional motion¶

Instanteanous speed is defined by the specific displacement in a speccific time time ratio like as:

\begin{equation} v = \dfrac{\Delta x}{\Delta t} = \dfrac{x_2-x_1}{t_2-t_1} \end{equation}

so, the change of velocity as a function of time define acceleration (instantanoeus) , then : \begin{equation} a = \dfrac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \dfrac{v_2-v_1}{t_2-t_1} \end{equation}

Compute instantanous speed¶

From eq.(1) we can compute the instantanoeus speed in each instant of time, then : \begin{equation} v_{1\to 2} = \dfrac{x_2-x_1}{t_2-t_1} = \dfrac{4.1-0}{5-0} = 0.82 \text{cm/s} \end{equation}

Compute instantanous acceleration¶

From eq.(2) we can compute the instantaneous acceleration in each instant of time, then : \begin{equation} a_{1\to 2} = \dfrac{v_2-v_1}{t_2-t_1} = \dfrac{5-0}{4.1-0} = -0.012000 \approx 0 \text{cm/s} \end{equation}
